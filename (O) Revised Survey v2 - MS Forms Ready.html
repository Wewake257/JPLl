🧩 Section C – Behavioral Scenarios 3 Scenarios per Level · Level-Adapted Rank options from 1 (most effective) to 4 (least effective)
👤 Junior / Executive – Scenarios
For each situation, rank the 4 options from 1 = most effective to 4 = least effective. Use each rank exactly once. Choose what YOU would actually do — not the "textbook" answer.
Scenario 1 · Report Error
You are finishing a report due tomorrow morning. At 6 PM you find a big data error that affects the whole analysis. Your manager has already left.
A. Fix only the parts you are confident about and submit on time.
B. Contact your manager right away — explain the problem and agree on the best next step.
C. Submit as-is tonight and plan to fix the error in the next version.
D. Stay as long as it takes, fix the full report, and send your manager a clear summary of changes.
MS Forms: Add as a Ranking question. Each option gets a rank 1–4. All ranks must be used once.
Scenario 2 · Helping a Colleague
A close colleague is behind on a big deliverable due tomorrow and asks for your help. You also have deadlines, but yours are tight — not impossible.
A. Help your colleague fully — team success matters more right now.
B. Finish your own work first, then help with whatever time is left.
C. Help with the most critical parts and reduce the scope of your own deliverable to compensate.
D. Suggest your colleague speak to your manager to get extra support.
MS Forms: Add as a Ranking question. Ranks 1–4, used exactly once each.
Scenario 3 · Feedback You Disagree With
Your manager tells you that you work too independently and do not communicate with the team enough. You partly disagree.
A. Calmly explain your side and why the feedback may not be accurate.
B. Listen carefully, ask for specific examples, genuinely reflect on whether there is truth in it, then share your perspective.
C. Nod along to avoid conflict and continue working the same way.
D. Ask a trusted colleague privately if they agree before responding to your manager.
MS Forms: Add as a Ranking question. Ranks 1–4, used exactly once each.
👥 Manager / Team Lead – Scenarios
For each situation, rank the 4 options from 1 = most effective to 4 = least effective. Use each rank exactly once. Choose what YOU would actually do as a manager.
Scenario 1 · Team Member Struggling
A team member who was doing well has recently become disengaged — missing deadlines, going quiet in meetings, and looking withdrawn. This has been going on for about three weeks.
A. Watch for another two to three weeks to see if things improve on their own.
B. Have a private, honest conversation — understand what changed, listen without judging, and build a plan together.
C. Escalate to HR immediately and formally document the performance concern.
D. Quietly reassign their work to other team members while the situation resolves.
MS Forms: Add as a Ranking question. Ranks 1–4, used exactly once each.
Scenario 2 · Promotion Decision
Two of your best team members are both ready for the only promotion available this cycle. Their performance records are almost equal.
A. Promote the one with the slightly higher rating — let the data decide.
B. Promote the one who is more of a team player — culture fit matters more long-term.
C. Give both a stretch project for 90 days before deciding — let the assignment show the difference.
D. Ask HR or your manager to make the final call — keeping it objective protects everyone.
MS Forms: Add as a Ranking question. Ranks 1–4, used exactly once each.
Scenario 3 · Team Resisting Change
A new organization-wide initiative requires your team to replace a process they have used successfully for two years. Your team is openly against the change.
A. Push through firmly — the organization has decided and you cannot wait any longer.
B. Break the initiative into clear steps and run a session with the team to address concerns and build the plan together.
C. Delay implementation until the team's resistance fades on its own.
D. Let each person implement the change in their own way and time to protect morale.
MS Forms: Add as a Ranking question. Ranks 1–4, used exactly once each.

Scenario 1 · Teams Working in Silos
A critical project milestone was missed because two teams operated independently with different assumptions about who owned a key decision.
A. Identify who was mainly responsible and hold them formally accountable.
B. Bring both teams together immediately, find the root cause, and redesign how they coordinate going forward.
C. Accept the delay, update the timeline, and monitor more closely — without changing the structure.
D. Bring in external consultants to review and redesign the cross-team operating model.

Scenario 2 · Cost Reduction Decision
You must cut costs by 15% this quarter. Option A: Targeted headcount cuts in lower-priority areas. Option B: Proportional cuts across all teams equally.
A. Make targeted cuts in lower-priority areas to protect your highest-value capabilities.
B. Apply proportional cuts equally across all teams to protect morale and culture.
C. Delay by 60 days to gather more data before committing.
D. Present both options transparently to the leadership team and make the decision together.
MS Forms: Add as a Ranking question. Ranks 1–4, used exactly once each.
Scenario 3 · Unrealistic Stakeholder Pressure
A key external stakeholder is pushing you to commit to a project outcome your internal data clearly shows is not achievable within the current timeline and resources.
A. Agree to the commitment to protect the relationship and negotiate scope privately later.
B. Present your data clearly, explain what is realistically achievable, and offer an alternative that addresses the stakeholder's actual goal.
C. Escalate to your own manager — agreeing could set a bad organizational precedent.
D. Commit publicly and push the team hard to try to deliver.

✍️ Section D – Personal Reflection 2 Open-Ended Questions · Level-Adapted · 30-Word Minimum Most important section — captures what structured questions cannot
👤 Junior / Executive
Answer both questions in your own words. Write at least 30 words per answer. Be specific — describe what you personally did, not what the team did.
D1 · Achievement & Impact
Tell us about one piece of work you are genuinely proud of. What did you do specifically, and what changed because of it?
📝 Open text · Minimum 30 words · Focus on YOUR actions and the result
D2 · Growth & Self-Awareness
What is one thing you are actively working to get better at right now? What are you actually doing about it?
📝 Open text · Minimum 30 words · Be honest — there are no wrong answers here
👥 Manager / Team Lead
Answer both questions in your own words. Write at least 30 words per answer. Be specific — describe what you personally did as a manager, not what your team did.
D1 · Team Impact
Tell us about a time when something you did as a manager made a real positive difference for your team or a direct report. What did you do, and what changed?
📝 Open text · Minimum 30 words · Focus on YOUR actions as a manager and the outcome
D2 · Leadership Growth
What is one area where you know your management style needs to improve? What are you actively doing to work on it?
📝 Open text · Minimum 30 words · Be candid — this helps us understand your self-awareness as a leader
🏛️ Leader / VP+
Answer both questions in your own words. Write at least 30 words per answer. Think at the organizational level — describe decisions or initiatives that had broad impact.
D1 · Organizational Impact
Tell us about a leadership decision or initiative you are proud of that had a meaningful impact beyond your immediate team. What did you decide or do, and what was the result?
📝 Open text · Minimum 30 words · Focus on organizational-level impact, not just team-level outcomes
D2 · Leadership Evolution
What is one way you have had to change your leadership approach in the last 12 months — and what drove that change?
📝 Open text · Minimum 30 words · Be reflective — this shows how you learn and adapt as a leader
