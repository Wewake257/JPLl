Why This Project Exists
The Problem We Are Solving
Three critical gaps in the current HR talent management approach
❌
No Standard Success Profile
No defined benchmark for what 'excellent' looks like in key roles. Hiring and promotions rely on gut feel.
📊
Inconsistent Performance Ratings
Ratings vary across managers and departments with no structured basis, making objective comparison impossible.
⚠️
Reactive Talent Decisions
No data-backed tool to proactively identify at-risk or high-potential employees. Decisions are made reactively.
🎯 Objective: Move from intuition-based talent decisions to data-driven performance prediction.
Project Foundation

Building a data‑driven competency model to predict high performance

Methodology Behind
Frameworks Powering This Model
Three globally recognized frameworks integrated into one cohesive model — used across all surveys and interviews
🧊
ICEBERG FRAMEWORK
Spencer & Spencer
Identifies underlying drivers — both visible (knowledge, skills) and hidden (traits, motives). Guides survey & interview design.
📋
67 COMPETENCIES
Lominger Architect
Converts broad leadership requirements into specific, measurable competency language — used to frame interview questions.
🎯
BEHAVIORAL INDICATORS
SHL OPQ
Defines observable behavioral anchors for each competency — forms the basis of Likert items in Survey‑2 and STAR probes.
🔁 All three frameworks inform the design of Survey‑1, STAR interviews, and Survey‑2 — ensuring alignment from initial data collection to final prediction.

Roadblocks
The Challenges We Face
Current hurdles and methodological constraints
📊 No Historical Behavioral Data
OLP scores exist for 920 employees, but no data on behaviours. Survey-1 built from scratch.
📱 Microsoft Forms Limitations
No native Semantic Differential or drag-and-drop ranking support.
⚠️ Response Rate Risk
150+ responses in 2-day window requires strong manager advocacy.
🧠 Self-Report Bias
All data is self-reported. No external validation (manager ratings, peer feedback).
📋 Sample Representation
Uneven response rates across job levels will create unequal reliability across hierarchy.
